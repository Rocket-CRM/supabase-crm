## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company is engaged in the business of developing and operating a B2C CRM platform, including but not limited to loyalty & membership programs, integrated chat solutions, customer service software, and marketing automation software;

WHEREAS, the Employee is employed by or will be employed by the Company in a position that requires access to Confidential Information, trade secrets, and proprietary business knowledge;

WHEREAS, the Company has a legitimate business interest in protecting its Confidential Information, client relationships, and competitive position;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the Employee's employment, compensation, and access to Confidential Information, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## SECTION 1 — DEFINITIONS

**1.1 "Confidential Information"** means any and all non-public information, in any form or medium, whether written, oral, electronic, or visual, relating to the Company's business, including but not limited to:

(a) **Product & Technology Information** — product features, roadmaps, specifications, software source code, algorithms, system architecture, platform technology stack, infrastructure design, database schemas, API designs, technical documentation, and development methodologies;

(b) **Business & Commercial Information** — business plans, strategies, financial data, pricing models, revenue figures, cost structures, profit margins, budgets, forecasts, and investment plans;

(c) **Sales & Marketing Information** — sales strategies, marketing plans, marketing materials, campaign data, conversion metrics, customer acquisition costs, promotional strategies, and market research;

(d) **Client & Customer Information** — client lists, customer databases, customer profiles, customer usage data, customer communication history, contract terms, service agreements, and any information related to existing or prospective clients;

(e) **Operational Information** — internal processes, operational procedures, vendor and supplier information, partnership agreements, integration specifications, and service level agreements;

(f) **Human Resources Information** — employee compensation data, organizational structure, hiring plans, and internal performance assessments;

(g) **Intellectual Property** — patents (pending or granted), trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets, inventions, designs, and any proprietary methodologies or frameworks;

(h) **Any other information** that the Company designates as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

**1.2 "Competitors"** means the entities listed in Section 3.2 and any other entity operating in the fields described therein.

**1.3 "Restricted Period"** means a period of two (2) years from the date of termination of the Employee's employment with the Company, regardless of the reason for termination.

**1.4 "Territory"** means the Kingdom of Thailand and any country in which the Company operates or has active clients at the time of the Employee's termination.

---

## SECTION 2 — CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS

**2.1 Non-Disclosure.** The Employee agrees that, both during the term of employment and at all times thereafter (without time limitation), the Employee shall:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;

(b) Not disclose, publish, or otherwise disseminate any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Company;

(c) Not use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than the performance of the Employee's duties for the Company;

(d) Take all reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure or use of Confidential Information.

**2.2 Exceptions.** The obligations in Section 2.1 shall not apply to information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault or action of the Employee;

(b) Was known to the Employee prior to disclosure by the Company, as evidenced by written records;

(c) Is independently developed by the Employee without reference to the Confidential Information;

(d) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the Employee gives the Company prompt written notice of such requirement and cooperates with the Company in seeking a protective order.

**2.3 Return of Materials.** Upon termination of employment for any reason, or upon the Company's request at any time, the Employee shall immediately return to the Company all documents, files, records, devices, and any other materials (whether physical or electronic) containing or relating to Confidential Information. The Employee shall not retain any copies, extracts, or summaries thereof. This includes but is not limited to:

(a) Company-issued laptops, phones, and storage devices;

(b) All files stored on personal devices, cloud accounts, or external storage;

(c) Printed documents, notes, and any reproductions of Confidential Information.

**2.4 Ongoing Obligation.** The Employee acknowledges that the confidentiality obligations under this Section 2 survive the termination of employment indefinitely and are not limited by the Restricted Period defined in Section 1.3.

## SECTION 3 — NON-COMPETE OBLIGATIONS

**3.1 Non-Compete Covenant.** During the Restricted Period, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, whether as an employee, consultant, advisor, officer, director, shareholder, partner, agent, or in any other capacity:

(a) Be employed by, provide services to, or be affiliated in any way with any Competitor;

(b) Establish, invest in, operate, manage, or otherwise participate in any business that competes with the Company's business;

(c) Assist any person or entity in establishing or operating a business that competes with the Company.

**3.2 Identified Competitors.** The following entities are identified as Competitors under this Agreement. This list is illustrative and non-exhaustive:

**3.3 Competitive Fields.** In addition to the named Competitors above, this non-compete restriction extends to any company, whether existing or newly formed, that operates in any of the following fields:

(a) B2C CRM platforms (member management and loyalty programs);

(b) Integrated chat solutions for business-to-consumer communication;

(c) Customer service and helpdesk software;

(d) Marketing automation software targeting B2C businesses;

(e) Any combination or substantial overlap of the above fields.

**3.4 Permitted Activities.** The restrictions in this Section 3 shall not prohibit the Employee from:

(a) Holding a passive investment of less than 5% of the publicly traded shares of any company;

(b) Working in a division or subsidiary of a large organization that is not engaged in the competitive fields described above, provided there is a clear separation of duties and no access to competitive operations.

## SECTION 4 — NON-SOLICITATION

**4.1 Non-Solicitation of Clients.** During the Restricted Period, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, contact, or attempt to divert any client or prospective client of the Company with whom the Employee had contact or about whom the Employee received Confidential Information during the last twelve (12) months of employment.

**4.2 Non-Solicitation of Employees.** During the Restricted Period, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, recruit, solicit, or induce any employee, contractor, or consultant of the Company to leave the Company's service or to breach any agreement with the Company.

---

## SECTION 5 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**5.1 Work Product Ownership.** All inventions, discoveries, designs, developments, improvements, software, documentation, and other works of authorship (collectively, "Work Product") conceived, created, or developed by the Employee during the term of employment and related to the Company's business shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company.

**5.2 Assignment.** The Employee hereby assigns and transfers to the Company all rights, title, and interest in and to any Work Product created during the term of employment. The Employee agrees to execute any documents reasonably requested by the Company to perfect such assignment.

**5.3 Moral Rights.** To the extent permitted by law, the Employee waives any moral rights in the Work Product.

---

## SECTION 6 — LIQUIDATED DAMAGES AND REMEDIES

**6.1 Liquidated Damages.** The Employee acknowledges that a breach of any obligation under Sections 2, 3, or 4 of this Agreement would cause the Company substantial and irreparable harm that may be difficult to quantify. Accordingly, the Parties agree that, in the event of a breach of any provision of this Agreement, the Employee shall pay the Company liquidated damages in an amount equal to no less than **six (6) months of the Employee's gross monthly salary** at the time of termination, without prejudice to the Company's right to claim additional damages if the actual harm suffered by the Company exceeds such amount as determined by a damage assessment.

**6.2 Serious Breach and Immediate Termination.** The Employee acknowledges and agrees that any breach of the obligations under Sections 2, 3, or 4 of this Agreement shall constitute a **serious breach of the employment contract** (ความผิดร้ายแรง) within the meaning of Section 119 of the Thai Labour Protection Act B.E. 2541 (1998). In the event of such breach, the Company shall have the right to:

(a) **Terminate the Employee's employment immediately** without prior notice or warning;

(b) **Withhold severance pay** as permitted under Section 119 of the Labour Protection Act;

(c) **Withhold payment in lieu of advance notice** as the termination is for cause;

(d) Pursue all remedies under this Agreement, including but not limited to liquidated damages under Section 6.1 and additional damages under Section 6.3.

The Employee understands that the obligations under this Agreement are fundamental to the employment relationship and that the Company's trust in the Employee's compliance is a material condition of employment.

**6.3 Additional Damages.** The liquidated damages specified in Section 6.1 represent a minimum threshold and shall not limit the Company's right to pursue compensation for the full extent of actual damages, including but not limited to loss of business, loss of clients, loss of competitive advantage, and any consequential damages arising from the breach, as may be established through a damage assessment or as determined by a court of competent jurisdiction.

**6.4 Injunctive Relief.** In addition to liquidated damages, the Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief from any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent or restrain any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement, without the need to post a bond or prove actual damages.

**6.5 Cumulative Remedies.** The remedies provided in this Agreement are cumulative and are in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity. The exercise of one remedy shall not preclude the exercise of any other remedy.

**6.6 Legal Costs.** In the event of a breach, the breaching party shall bear all reasonable legal costs, attorney fees, and expenses incurred by the non-breaching party in enforcing this Agreement.

## SECTION 7 — GENERAL PROVISIONS

**7.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Kingdom of Thailand.

**7.2 Dispute Resolution.** Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be submitted to the Labor Court of Thailand having jurisdiction over the matter.

**7.3 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**7.4 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether oral or written.

**7.5 Amendment.** This Agreement may only be amended or modified by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

**7.6 Waiver.** The failure of either Party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of such provision or of the right to enforce it at a later time.

**7.7 Assignment.** The Company may assign this Agreement to any successor or affiliate. The Employee may not assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the Company.

**7.8 Survival.** Sections 2 (Confidentiality), 5 (Intellectual Property), and 6 (Remedies) shall survive the termination of this Agreement and the Employee's employment indefinitely. Sections 3 (Non-Compete) and 4 (Non-Solicitation) shall survive for the duration of the Restricted Period.

**7.9 Acknowledgment.** The Employee acknowledges that:

(a) The Employee has read, understood, and voluntarily agreed to the terms of this Agreement;

(b) The restrictions in this Agreement are reasonable and necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests;

(c) The Employee has had the opportunity to seek independent legal advice before signing this Agreement;

(d) The Employee's compensation and continued employment constitute adequate consideration for the obligations under this Agreement.
